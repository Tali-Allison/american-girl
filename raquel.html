Raquel's story

Raquel Reyes is a 10-year-old girl living in Kansas City, Missouri, with her family. Her dad is Mexican American and her family owns the best paleta shop in the city, Paleta-Palooza. Every year on the first day of summer the shop introduces two new flavors, drawing long lines of customers. Raquel is learning how to DJ and will be bringing “la música” to the event.

Raquel is also excited but nervous about her summer plans—visiting family on the East Coast and attending a family wedding. She’s most looking forward to becoming closer to her cousin Sloan, who is just a year older. But when Sloan starts acting distant, Raquel is determined to figure out what’s wrong. With the help of a long-lost family diary, an unexpected animal rescue, and a whole lot of heart, Raquel discovers that family isn’t just about history—it’s about showing up for the people who need you most.

Family & friends

Leo & Julia Reyes

Raquel’s parents, who own a popular paleta shop in Kansas City. Her mom is a descendant of Samantha Parkington and her dad is Mexican American.

Sol Reyes

Raquel’s energetic and playful 7-year-old brother. He’s full of confidence and loves performing, especially alongside their dog, Luzita.

Luzita

Raquel’s beloved Pomeranian, a small but brave rescue dog. She is the official taste-tester for all of the shop’s dog-safe flavors.

Sloan

Raquel’s cousin, who is one year older. She is a great pickleball player, loves drawing, and dreams of creating graphic novels.

Harper

Raquel’s oldest cousin, a marine biologist. She and her fiancé organize the dolphin rescue and inspire Raquel.

Jordan

Harper’s fiancé, also a marine biologist. He helps educate the family about dolphins and assists with the rescue operation.

Liam

A 14-year-old cousin who comes out of his shell during the family reunion. Liam is an avid pickleball player.

Grandma Meg

The family’s matriarch on Raquel’s mom’s side. She owns an old mansion in Mount Bedford that’s filled with family history.

Abuela Rosie

Raquel’s grandmother on her dad’s side. She stays behind to run Paleta-Palooza while the family travels.

Samantha Parkington

Raquel’s great-great-grandmother, whose diary Raquel discovers in the old family mansion. Her words help Raquel understand family in a deeper way.

Fun facts

  • Raquel’s favorite animal, the dolphin, is highly social and swims in groups call pods.
  • La música is a large part of Raquel’s Mexican heritage. She grew up listening to Latin music hits with her abuela Rosie, from cumbias to classic Mexican ballads.
  • Paletas are a frozen confection of fresh tropical fruit infused with traditional Mexican flavors like lime, cinnamon, and red chile. Raquel’s favorite flavor is watermelon.
  • Pickleball is a social sport—perfect for two or four players. Serves are performed underhand, and the ball must bounce once on each side before players can begin to volley competitively.
  • Raquel’s family travels to a beach town on Long Island Sound called Sandy Point Harbor for her family reunion. It’s a charming seaside town on the east coast.
  • Raquel and Harper visit an old family mansion in Mount Bedford, New York—the same place Samantha Parkington grew up in in the early 1900s.
